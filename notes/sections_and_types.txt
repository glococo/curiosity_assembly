Quick Summary Table – Where Sections End Up

Section       , Ends up in, Copied at startup?, Cleared at startup? , Typical use case
.text         , Flash     , No                , —                   , Program code
.rodata       , Flash     , No                , —                   , const data
.progmem.data , Flash     , No                , —                   , PROGMEM arrays/strings
.data         , SRAM      , Yes               , No                  , Initialized variables in C, not ASM
.bss          , SRAM      , No                , Yes (zeroed)        , Global variables initialized in C, not aSM 
.noinit       , SRAM      , No                , No                  , Persistent across reset
.eeprom       , EEPROM    , No                , No                  , "Configuration, counters"
.fuse/.lock   , Fuse/Lock , No                , No                  , MCU configuration


Examples – How They Look in Assembly

.section .text                            ; code goes here
    jmp main

.section .rodata
msg: .string "Hello AVR!"

.section .progmem.data                    ; classic PROGMEM style
my_table: .byte 1,2,3,4

.section .data
    .global counter
counter: .word 1234                       ; initialized → copied from flash

.section .bss
    .lcomm big_buffer, 512                ; uninitialized, zeroed
    .space  32

.section .noinit
persistent_flag: .byte 0                  ; survives reset

.section .fuse
    .byte 0xFF                            ; example fuse low byte


Overview of the most common data definition / allocation directives used in AVR assembly
 when using the GNU assembler (avr-as / gas) — the default in AVR-GCC toolchains.
These directives tell the assembler how to place constants, strings, or reserve space in
 the current section (.text, .data, .bss, .rodata, .progmem.data, .eeprom, etc.).

; Constants in Flash / PROGMEM
.section .progmem.data
    my_byte:     .byte   42               ; one byte = 0x2A
    my_word:     .word   0x1234           ; bytes: 34 12 (little-endian)
    status_msg:  .asciz  "Sensor OK"      ; bytes + 0x00 at end
    raw_text:    .ascii  "Raw"            ; no trailing zero

; Initialized variables in SRAM (.data → copied from flash at startup)
.section .data
    counter:     .word   1000             ; 16-bit initialized variable
    flag:        .byte   1
    version_str: .string "v1.2.3"         ; usually null-terminated

; Uninitialized (zeroed) in SRAM (.bss)
.section .bss
    buffer:      .space  128              ; 128 bytes, zeroed at startup
    big_array:   .skip   512              ; synonym
    temp:        .lcomm  4                ; 4 bytes, local symbol

; EEPROM example
.section .eeprom
    eemagic:     .word   0xA5A5
    eestring:    .asciz  "Config"
